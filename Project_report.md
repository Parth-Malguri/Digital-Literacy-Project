## Introduction

This project was assigned as part of the Digital Literacy course, where I took on the role of a Student Digital Ambassador. The objective was to build a Digital Literacy Portfolio to help students understand essential digital skills required in today’s world.

The project consisted of five tasks: creating a digital literacy infographic, building a professional digital portfolio, exploring coding and collaboration platforms, understanding email etiquette, and studying cybercrime awareness. Each task helped develop practical skills such as online communication, safe internet usage, and professional digital presence.

Overall, this project provided hands-on exposure to important digital tools and concepts that are useful for both academic and professional growth.

## Task 1 — Digital Literacy Awareness Infographic

**Tool Used:** Canva

**What My Design Shows:**

The infographic I created is meant to be a thorough guide for students as they move through the complex digital environment of today. It is based on what I think are the three most important areas: knowledge, practice, and safety. The first part I dealt with students' thinking and learning skills related to digital literacy. I showed that being digitally literate means more than just going online; rather it involves having the advanced skills to efficiently access, critically evaluate and creatively produce digital content. In order to help students to put into practice what they learn first about digital literacy, the second section of the infographic introduces some professional tools such as Google Workspace, GitHub, and LinkedIn to demonstrate how these platforms enable the kind of collaboration and creativity that are fundamental in career building nowadays. As a foundation, I have placed the entire infographic inside a firm security practice. I argue that knowing how to use technology won't be a lasting skill without the first priority mindset through safety. This means the implementation of good passwords, two-factor authentication, and a careful eye when it comes to phishing and data privacy. With this project, I wanted to convey that digital literacy is far from a single skill it is a comprehensive set of tools for a student to be able to survive in today's world.

**Reflection:**

One thing I found interesting was how visual design can make information easier to understand compared to plain text. However, I found it slightly difficult to balance content and design, as adding too much text made the infographic look crowded. This task helped me improve both my understanding of digital literacy and my ability to present information effectively.

## Task 5 — Cybercrime Awareness Case Study

Cybercrime Type Researched: Ransomware

What Surprised Me Most:

I was genuinely shocked to discover the rise of "Ransomware-as-a-Service" (RaaS), where developers actually sell easy-to-use kits to amateur criminals for a cut of the profit. It’s no longer just elite hackers; it’s a professionalized industry with help desks and "double extortion" tactics where they threaten to leak my private files even if I don't care about getting the encrypted ones back.

One Habit I Will Personally Change:

I am going to start following the "3-2-1" backup rule immediately—keeping three copies of my data on two different types of media, with one copy completely offline. I realized that keeping my backup drive plugged in all the time is a huge mistake, as ransomware can travel through the cable and lock my backups too, so I’ll be keeping my external drive disconnected unless I’m actively syncing it.
