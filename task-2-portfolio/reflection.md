Project Report: Strategic Use of LinkedIn, Kaggle, and GitHub
For an engineering degree, LinkedIn, Kaggle, and GitHub were selected as the three most impactful digital platforms because they directly support skill-building, collaboration, and career readiness—core requirements.
LinkedIn is a professional networking site used to build industry connections, showcase internships, projects, and certifications, and discover job/internship opportunities.
Kaggle is a data-science community platform for participating in machine-learning competitions, exploring real-world datasets, and publishing Jupyter notebooks to demonstrate analytical skills.
GitHub serves as a version-control and code-hosting platform where students store, collaborate on, and publicly display engineering projects, making it the standard portfolio tool for recruiters and open-source contributors.
Four-Year Utilization Plan

Year 1: Create polished profiles on all three; complete beginner Kaggle courses and upload first coding assignments to GitHub; connect with 50+ alumni on LinkedIn.
Year 2: Join entry-level Kaggle competitions, contribute to small open-source repositories, and start posting project updates on LinkedIn.
Year 3: Lead group projects on GitHub, publish competition kernels, and use LinkedIn to secure summer internships/research positions.
Year 4: Showcase the final-year capstone on GitHub and LinkedIn, actively network for full-time roles, and maintain an updated portfolio that reflects four years of progressive engineering growth.

This structured approach transforms the three platforms into a powerful professional ecosystem throughout the degree.
