'Stay Safe Online' Checklist for StudentsL:

Secure Your UPI Transactions: Always remember that entering your UPI PIN is only for sending money, never for receiving it. If someone sends you a QR code claiming you need to scan it to "receive a refund" or "scholarship," it is a scam.

Verify Official Apps: Only download banking and UPI apps (like BHIM, Google Pay, or PhonePe) from the official Google Play Store or Apple App Store. Avoid "modded" versions of apps that promise extra features.

Be Cautious with Campus/Public Wi-Fi: Avoid logging into your bank accounts or student portals over unsecured public Wi-Fi. If you must use it, ensure you are using a VPN to encrypt your data.

Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA): Activate 2FA on your university email, WhatsApp, and social media accounts. Use an authenticator app rather than SMS-based codes when possible to prevent SIM-swapping attacks.

Spot "Urgent" Phishing Scams: Be skeptical of emails or WhatsApp messages claiming your "Electricity will be cut off" or "Exam registration expires in 1 hour." These create artificial panic to make you click malicious links.

Practice Clean Social Media Habits: Avoid sharing your Aadhaar number, PAN card, or photos of your admit cards/hall tickets on Instagram or Snapchat. This information is a goldmine for identity thieves.

Use Strong, Unique Passwords: Use a password manager to ensure you aren't reusing the same password for your "Student Management System" (SMS) and your personal Gmail.

Check Link Authenticity: Before clicking, hover over links to see the actual URL. Scammers often use "typo-squatting" (e.g., g0ogle.com instead of https://www.google.com/search?q=google.com) to trick you into entering credentials on a fake page.

If You Are Targeted: How to Report
If you experience a cyber-financial fraud or any online harassment, immediate reporting is critical to freezing the stolen funds or removing harmful content.

Dial the National Helpline: Call 1930 immediately for emergency assistance with financial fraud.

File an Official Complaint: Visit the National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal at [cybercrime.gov.in] to register a formal case.

Preserve Evidence: Take screenshots of the transaction ID, the offender’s profile, or the fraudulent messages before they are deleted.
