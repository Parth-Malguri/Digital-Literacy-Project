Case Study: The Healthcare Breach 
Ransomware is a type of malicious software designed to block access to a computer system or encrypt its data until a sum of money is paid. While attackers target various sectors, the healthcare industry is a frequent victim because the urgency of patient care creates immense pressure to restore systems quickly.

How It Happens: Step-by-Step
Infiltration: An employee at a regional hospital receives a "spear-phishing" email disguised as an urgent medical supply invoice. Clicking the attached PDF executes a hidden script.

Lateral Movement: Once inside the network, the malware communicates with a server. It silently spreads across the hospital’s servers, seeking administrative credentials and identifying sensitive patient databases.

Exfiltration and Encryption: Before locking the files, the attackers steal private patient records to use as secondary leverage. Finally, the ransomware encrypts the hospital’s Electronic Health Records and backup servers.

The Ransom Note: Hospital staff find their screens replaced by a digital note demanding $500,000 in Bitcoin for the decryption key, threatening to leak patient data if the deadline passes.

Consequences
The impact is devastating. Medical staff lose access to patient histories, allergy information, and surgical schedules, forcing a pivot to manual paper records and delaying life-saving treatments. Beyond the immediate chaos, the hospital faces massive financial losses from downtime, potential legal fines for data breaches, and a long-term loss of community trust.
